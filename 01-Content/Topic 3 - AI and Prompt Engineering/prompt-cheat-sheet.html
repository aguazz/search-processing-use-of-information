Prompt & Technique Cheat Sheet

The one page to keep. Two frameworks for asking well, the checklist behind them, the techniques that sharpen any prompt, and how to ask for an Artifact — for this course and the Group Project after it.

Start here — ICC or AIM?

ICC — creating from scratch. No source material yet: an email, a summary, an idea. You supply everything.
AIM — working on material you have. A document, dataset, or draft to analyze, review, or improve. You direct an expert over it.

ICC drafting from scratch

Instructions
The exact task — one clear action.
Context
Your role, your objective, and who the output is for.
Constraints
Style, tone, length, format — plus an example to imitate, if you have one.
Instructions: draft a short email asking for a deadline extension. Context: first-year student, missed 3 days ill, note available. Constraints: under 100 words, formal, no excuses beyond the illness.

AIM directing an expert

Actor
Who Claude should be — an expert role.
Input
The material to work from (attach or name it).
Mission
The ultra-specific, measurable goal — not "make it better".
Actor: a recruiter who has screened thousands of CVs. Input: my attached CV. Mission: the 5 weakest things for a data-analyst internship, each with a one-line fix.

The checklist what a strong prompt almost always has

  • A clear task (one action verb)
  • A role (who Claude is / who you are)
  • Context & audience (who it's for)
  • Material to work from → signals AIM
  • Constraints & format (length, tone, shape)
  • An example to imitate, if one exists

Techniques layer onto ICC / AIM

  • Let AI interview you: add "Ask me whatever questions you need to do a better job."
  • Reverse prompting: can't describe it? Hand over an example and let AI write the prompt.
  • Few-shot: show 2–3 examples instead of describing — it copies the pattern.
  • Step-by-step: ask it to reason in stages before answering — catches rushed errors.
  • Output shape: name the exact form — a table, 4 bullets, a template — not just "content".

Asking for an Artifact interactive output

  • Ask explicitly for an interactive Artifact — "make it a self-scoring quiz", not just "give me questions".
  • Ask from inside your Project so it draws on your real sources.
  • Name the type: quiz, flashcard deck, diagram, checklist.
  • It's yours to keep — download it or leave it in the Project.

Two things that beat any trick

Ground it
Give Claude the real source before you ask — an answer it can trace to your document beats one it invents. Bad data in, bad result out, no matter the model.
You decide
AI does the work; you keep the judgment. Verify anything that counts — numbers, names, citations — and be upfront about how you used it.

Stop speaking AI's language. Make AI work to understand you.
